# Research Focus

Prioritize the following angles when summarizing any paper.

## 1. Embodied AI and VLA

The user is a PhD student in embodied AI and works on large models at Galbot. They care about:

- VLA-related directions
- progress on embodied foundation models or smaller embodied models across companies and labs
- what a paper implies for practical embodied model design, training, or deployment

## 2. Video Generation for Embodied Policy

The user is especially interested in how video generation success can transfer into embodied intelligence, including:

- turning a video generation model into an embodied policy with extra modules or interfaces
- whether video-generation-based approaches may outperform VLM-based VLA systems in some regimes
- how to exploit video generation models for planning, control, imagination, or latent rollout
- generating robot-arm videos and extracting planning signals or control-relevant structure from them

## 3. Manipulation and Data

The user also cares about manipulation-related techniques and alternative data sources, including:

- pi0 and similar manipulation directions
- UMI-related work
- human data
- simulation data
- cross-embodiment transfer
- data pipelines that improve manipulation performance or generalization

## 4. Broad Horizon

Do not stay too narrow. The user wants both:

- hands-on projects in concrete embodied subareas
- broad exposure beyond a single niche

It is valuable to connect ideas from outside embodied AI back into embodied use cases, especially:

- video generation models
- large-model training methods
- scalable data generation or filtering pipelines
- transferable optimization, architecture, or evaluation ideas

## Summary Preference

The user wants to know quickly:

- what the paper's final conclusion is
- what is genuinely new
- which parts matter for their research agenda
- whether the paper is worth a deeper read

Prefer selective emphasis over balanced but bland coverage.
